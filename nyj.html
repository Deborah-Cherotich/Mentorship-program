Born A Crime 1 & 2

In the book "Born a Crime" by Trevor Noah, chapter one touches on themes that resonate
with what's happening in Trans-Nzoia County currently. One of the themes that stood out
in the book is Apartheid, which exploits linguistic and tribalism, resulting in division
among communities, just like the Xhosa and Zulu. Trevor Noah comes from a mixed family
where his mother was Xhosa, and his father was a white Swiss man.

Another theme that the book highlights is religion. Noah's mother has faith, which makes her believe
in different churches which leads her to attend three different churches every Sunday, each offering
something different. The first church offers praise, the second church offers deep analysis of the
scripture, which Trevor's mum loves the most. In Trans-Nzoia County, we have different churches that
people believe offer something different.

In our county, there have been conflicts between drivers and passengers for various reasons.
For instance, Trevor's mother was threatened by a minibus driver which led to her being
thrown out of the car and even jumping out of a moving vehicle. Some drivers in our county
harass their passengers, speaking to them in a threatening manner, which has caused some
fatal accidents and injuries, and even resulted in loss of life.

In chapter two of the book, we see how native people were forced to leave their land and
were subjected to slavery. They lived in constant fear, and Trevor, being the product of
a white man and a black woman, was at risk of arrest due to community laws. Despite the
crime that she committed, Trevor's mother, who worked as a typist, was courageous and
feared no one. This resonates with our county, where people from different backgrounds
have been forced to work as slaves in other communities due to poverty.

In 2007, in Trans-Nzoia County, there was a division between the Maasai and the Luhya communities due
to social status. The Maasai started fighting the Luhya because they wanted them to migrate from the
land and leave it to them. This conflict brought out the theme of belonging, as seen in the book
"Born a Crime" where the Zulu warriors fought against the Xhosas because they felt the Xhosas had
power over them.

Another theme in Trans-Nzoia County is parents' love for their children. Children are
cared for and loved by their parents who pay for their education. Similarly, Trevor's
mother loved him so much that she didn't want him to interact with other people in the
community because they could isolate him due to his being a white child.
